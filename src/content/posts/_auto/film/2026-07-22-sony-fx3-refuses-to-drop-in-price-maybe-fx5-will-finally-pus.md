The Sony FX3 is stubborn. Not creatively. Financially. Sony launched the FX3 in 2021 as a compact full frame Cinema Line camera, with a European price of around €4,700 and a clear mission: bring Cinema Line image quality into a small Alpha-sized body. More than five years later, the strange part is not that the FX3 is still relevant. The strange part is that its price has barely softened in a meaningful way. That is rare in camera years. Most cameras age quickly. New processors arrive. Autofocus improves. Codecs expand. Screens get better. Monitoring tools become standard.
